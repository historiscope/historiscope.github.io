Fun with Bar Plots: Examining the Holdings

Posted on Sun 26 June 2016 in posts • Tagged with data science, python, projects, new york city, historical data, visualization, bar plots, directories

Using BeautifulSoup, I wrote a script to identify and gather the holdings information for the New York City directory collection in the Hathitrust catalog. The holdings information I scraped includes useful information about each directory, such as its title, publisher, publication year, and the total number of pages (really, files) in the digital version of the directory. With that information stored in my catalog data frame, I'm going to produce some tables to get a better sense of what all that scraping hath wrought. Specifically, I'd like to know how many directories I have for each year from 1800 to 1899, and how that collection breaks down by publisher and repository.
